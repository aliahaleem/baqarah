⭐ LEVEL 1 — THE 12 COSMIC SIGNS (81:1-7)
🎬 The Cosmic Signs — Click to reveal verses

Surah At-Takwir opens with one of the most dramatic descriptions of the Day of Judgment in the entire Quran — twelve impossible events listed one after another, each beginning with "Idha" (When).

The Arabic word "Kuwwirat" — "is rolled up" — for the sun comes from "kawwara," meaning to wrap a turban. The sun, that massive burning star that sustains all life on Earth, will be dimmed and wrapped away like a cloth.

The stars "inkadarat" — scatter and fall. Mountains "suyyirat" — set in motion, floating like wool. The most precious Arabian possessions — ten-month pregnant she-camels "'uttilat" — abandoned and neglected. Even wild animals are "hushirat" — gathered together.

These twelve signs build toward one conclusion in verse 14: "Alimat nafsun ma ahdharat" — A soul will know what it has prepared. Every cosmic structure designed for this world will be undone — and only your deeds remain.

إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انكَدَرَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا الْجِبَالُ سُيِّرَتْ
"When the sun is rolled up — when the stars fall scattered — when the mountains are set in motion." (81:1-3) — Three of the twelve cosmic signs. The permanence of the universe — dismantled.
وَإِذَا الْعِشَارُ عُطِّلَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا الْبِحَارُ سُجِّرَتْ
"When the pregnant she-camels are abandoned — when the wild animals are gathered — when the seas overflow/are set ablaze." (81:4-6) — Wealth abandoned, animals gathered, oceans overflow. No priority remains except standing before Allah.

👼 LEVEL 2 — THE BURIED GIRL (81:8-9)
🎬 The Vindication — Click to reveal verse

Among the twelve signs of the Day of Judgment, Allah inserts the most human of them — the case of Al-Maw'udah: the girl who was buried alive.

In pre-Islamic Arabia, some tribes — particularly those fearing poverty or feeling shame at a daughter's birth — would bury their newborn daughters alive. The Quran mentions this horror to condemn it in the strongest terms.

On the Day of Judgment, that buried girl will stand before Allah. And she will be asked: "Bi-ayyi dhanbin qutilat?" — For what SIN was she killed? The question is its own answer. There was no sin. She committed no crime. She only had the "fault" of being female and being born into a cruel household.

Allah gives her a voice before all of creation. Her testimony will stand against her killers. This is divine justice — the innocent given dignity, the oppressor held accountable.

Islam came to destroy this practice entirely — and elevated the status of daughters to be a source of barakah and mercy for the parents who raise them well.

وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ ۩ بِأَيِّ ذَنبٍ قُتِلَتْ
"And when the girl buried alive is asked — for what sin was she killed?" (81:8-9) — The girl is the one asked, not her killer. She is given voice and dignity. The very question is the indictment of those who murdered her.

📜 LEVEL 3 — WHAT HAS BEEN PREPARED (81:10-14)
🎬 The Final Revelation — Click to reveal verse

The twelve "Idha" statements continue — four more cosmic events leading to the climax of the entire surah.

"Wa idha al-suhuf nushirat" — the records are unrolled. Every deed ever done, every thought, every choice — exposed and spread open like an unrolled scroll.

"Wa idha al-sama' kushitat" — the sky is stripped away. The heavens themselves are peeled back, like removing a covering. Nothing is hidden anymore.

"Wa idha al-Jahim su''irat" — Hell is set ablaze. And "wa idha al-Jannah uzlifat" — Paradise is brought near, close enough to see and feel its proximity.

After all twelve events — "Alimat nafsun ma ahdharat" — A soul will KNOW what it has brought forward. All ambiguity, all excuses, all distractions — gone. The clarity of that moment is what drives a person to act NOW, while there is still time.

وَإِذَا الصُّحُفُ نُشِرَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا السَّمَاءُ كُشِطَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا الْجَحِيمُ سُعِّرَتْ ۩ وَإِذَا الْجَنَّةُ أُزْلِفَتْ ۩ عَلِمَتْ نَفْسٌ مَّا أَحْضَرَتْ
"When the records are laid open — when the sky is stripped away — when Hell is set ablaze — when Paradise is brought near — a soul will know what it has brought forward." (81:10-14) — The climax: KNOWING. Every soul gets a moment of complete, unchallengeable clarity.

👁️ LEVEL 4 — THE NOBLE ANGEL (81:19-21)
🎬 Jibril the Noble — Click to reveal verse

After describing the cosmic signs and the Day of Judgment, Allah now addresses the question the Quraysh kept asking: where does this Quran come from?

Allah establishes the chain of transmission: "Innahu la-qawlu rasulin karim" — Indeed it is the word of a noble Messenger. This "noble Messenger" is Jibril (AS), the Angel of Revelation.

He is described with four qualities: Karim (noble, generous in dignity), Dhi quwwah (of great power — he destroyed entire nations with one wing-beat), Maki'n 'inda dhi al-'Arsh (secure, established in high rank with the Owner of the Throne), and Muta' thamm amin (obeyed there, and trustworthy).

The message of the Quran comes through the most noble carrier possible. Think of how carefully we verify the chain of transmission for even a hadith — now imagine the care Allah placed in the carrier of His own direct words!

إِنَّهُ لَقَوْلُ رَسُولٍ كَرِيمٍ ۩ ذِي قُوَّةٍ عِندَ ذِي الْعَرْشِ مَكِينٍ ۩ مُّطَاعٍ ثَمَّ أَمِينٍ
"Indeed it is the word of a noble Messenger — of great power, secure in rank with the Owner of the Throne — obeyed there, and trustworthy." (81:19-21) — Four qualities of Jibril (AS): noble, powerful, high-ranked, obeyed by all angels, and trusted.

🌟 LEVEL 5 — YOUR COMPANION IS NOT MAD (81:22-25)
🎬 The Prophet Defended — Click to reveal verse

Having established that the Quran comes through a noble, trustworthy angel, Allah now directly defends the Prophet ﷺ against the slanders of Quraysh.

Defence 1: "Wa ma sahibukum bi-majnun" — Your COMPANION is not mad. Allah uses "sahibukum" — your companion, the one you know, who grew up among you, whose character you witnessed for 40 years before revelation. They knew he was not mad. Their accusation was dishonest.

Defence 2: "Wa la-qad ra'ahu bi-al-ufuq al-mubin" — He truly SAW Jibril on the clear, bright horizon. This was a real, verified sighting — not a hallucination or dream.

Defence 3: "Wa ma huwa 'ala al-ghayb bi-dhannin" — He is not withholding or miserly with knowledge of the unseen. He shares everything Allah reveals. He has no personal agenda.

Defence 4: "Wa ma huwa bi-qawli Shaytan rajim" — And it is NOT the word of a cursed devil. Shaytan is expelled, cursed, far from Allah. The Quran comes through Jibril — blessed, honoured, close to Allah. The contrast is total.

وَمَا صَاحِبُكُمْ بِمَجْنُونٍ ۩ وَلَقَدْ رَآهُ بِالْأُفُقِ الْمُبِينِ ۩ وَمَا هُوَ عَلَى الْغَيْبِ بِضَنِينٍ ۩ وَمَا هُوَ بِقَوْلِ شَيْطَانٍ رَّجِيمٍ
"Your companion is not mad — he certainly saw him on the clear horizon — he is not withholding knowledge of the unseen — and it is not the word of a cursed devil." (81:22-25) — Four defences, four slanders refuted in four sentences.

🌌 LEVEL 6 — WHERE ARE YOU GOING? (81:26-29)
🎬 The Straight Path — Click to reveal verse

The surah closes with one of the most compelling challenges in the Quran: "Fa-ayna tadh-habun?" — So where are you going?

It is not a geographical question. It is a question about direction of heart and life. You've been shown the cosmic signs of the Last Day. You've been told about the buried girl's vindication. You know what your records will show. You know the Quran comes through a noble angel to a truthful prophet. After ALL this — where exactly are you heading if not toward the truth?

"In huwa illa dhikrun lil-'alamin" — It is nothing but a reminder for ALL the worlds. Not just Arabs. Not just the 7th century. For all of creation, for all time.

"Li-man sha'a minkum an yastaqim" — For whoever WILLS among you to take the straight path. The will is yours. The choice is yours. The door is open.

Then the most humbling closing: "Wa ma tasha'una illa an yasha' Allah" — But you cannot will unless Allah wills. Your freedom is real — and it exists within Allah's greater will. This is not contradiction but completion: you have genuine choice, but you are not independent of your Creator.

فَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُونَ ۩ إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا ذِكْرٌ لِّلْعَالَمِينَ ۩ لِمَن شَاءَ مِنكُمْ أَن يَسْتَقِيمَ ۩ وَمَا تَشَاءُونَ إِلَّا أَن يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ
"So where are you going? It is nothing but a reminder to the worlds — for whoever wills among you to take a straight path. But you cannot will unless Allah wills — Lord of the Worlds." (81:26-29) — The surah's final call: choose the path, knowing your choice is within His will.
