⭐ LEVEL 1 — THE THREE OATHS (85:1-3)
🎬 The Constellation Sky — Click to reveal verse

Surah Al-Buruj begins with three divine oaths — sworn by Allah Himself — certifying the truth of everything that follows. When Allah swears, the oath carries infinite weight.

"Wa al-sama' dhat al-buruj" — By the sky possessing the constellations. Al-buruj are the great star formations — the towers of heaven. The vast, ordered, permanent constellations that humans have navigated by, worshipped wrongly, and marvelled at throughout history.

"Wa al-yawm al-maw'ud" — By the Promised Day. Allah swears by the very Day of Judgment that He promised — "maw'ud" means promised, guaranteed. If Allah swears by His own promise, how certain must that Day be?

"Wa shahidin wa mashhud" — By the witness and the witnessed. The most common interpretation: Friday (witness) and the Day of Arafah (witnessed); others say: the Prophet ﷺ (witness) and his community; or the angels (witness) and the deeds (witnessed). Human existence is being witnessed at every level.

وَالسَّمَاءِ ذَاتِ الْبُرُوجِ ۩ وَالْيَوْمِ الْمَوْعُودِ ۩ وَشَاهِدٍ وَمَشْهُودٍ
"By the sky with its constellations — by the Promised Day — by the witness and the witnessed." (85:1-3)

🔥 LEVEL 2 — THE PEOPLE OF THE DITCH (85:4-10)
🎬 The Burning Trenches — Click to reveal verse

The surah introduces one of the most powerful stories in the Quran about persecution of believers. Historical accounts identify this event as occurring in Yemen (around 525 CE), when the Jewish convert king Dhunuwas ordered that all Christians/monotheist believers be killed unless they apostatised.

"Qutila ashab al-ukhdud" — Destroyed/cursed were the People of the Ditch. "Ukhdud" is a deep trench dug into the earth — filled with fuel and fire. The believers were brought to the edge of these flaming trenches and given a choice: renounce your faith, or burn.

The believers chose the fire. They were thrown into the burning trenches. They died in flames rather than betray their faith in Allah.

What makes the description so chilling: "Idh hum 'alayha qu'ud — wa hum 'ala ma yaf'aluna bi-al-mu'minina shuhud" — while the persecutors sat over the ditches, WATCHING and WITNESSING what they were doing to the believers. They watched the burning with deliberate, calm witnessing. Their evil was conscious, purposeful, and public.

قُتِلَ أَصْحَابُ الْأُخْدُودِ ۩ النَّارِ ذَاتِ الْوَقُودِ ۩ إِذْ هُمْ عَلَيْهَا قُعُودٌ ۩ وَهُمْ عَلَىٰ مَا يَفْعَلُونَ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ شُهُودٌ
"Destroyed were the People of the Ditch — of the fire with burning fuel — while they were sitting over it — witnesses to what they were doing to the believers." (85:4-7)

✊ LEVEL 3 — THEIR ONLY CRIME: FAITH (85:8-9)
🎬 The Only Crime — Click to reveal verse

After describing the horrific persecution, the Quran asks the question that exposes the ultimate injustice: WHY did the persecutors hate the believers? What had they done?

"Wa ma naqamu minhum illa an yu'minu bi-Allah al-'Aziz al-Hamid." — They resented them for NOTHING — except that they believed in Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy.

That's it. No crime. No violence. No sedition. No harm to others. Just faith.

The divine names chosen are significant: Al-'Aziz — the Exalted in Might, the Invincible. The believers held onto the Most Powerful Being in existence, even as they faced apparent powerlessness. Al-Hamid — the Praiseworthy. They praised and glorified Allah even while being thrown into fire.

"Wa Allah 'ala kulli shay'in shahid" — Allah is witness over everything (85:9). The persecutors committed their evil thinking perhaps Allah was far away. He is not. He is witness over every single thing.

وَمَا نَقَمُوا مِنْهُمْ إِلَّا أَن يُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ ۩ الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ شَهِيدٌ
"They resented them for nothing except that they believed in Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy — to whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and earth — and Allah is witness over everything." (85:8-9)

🌿 LEVEL 4 — THE GREAT ATTAINMENT (85:11)
🎬 Gardens of Paradise — Click to reveal verse

Immediately after describing the terrible persecution, Allah declares the outcome for the believers: "Inna alladhina amanu wa 'amilu al-salihat lahum jannatun tajri min tahtiha al-anhar — dhalika al-fawz al-kabir."

Indeed those who believed and did righteous deeds will have gardens beneath which rivers flow — and that is AL-FAWZ AL-KABIR — the GREAT ATTAINMENT.

The word "fawz" means success, winning, attainment — getting what you sought. "Kabir" means great, vast, magnificent. Allah did not say small success, or partial success, or even just "success." He said GREAT success.

The contrast with the People of the Ditch is deliberate and powerful: The believers were thrown into fire on earth. In the next life, they receive gardens with RIVERS beneath them. Fire below → Water below. Earthly burning → Heavenly flowing. The reversal is complete and perfect.

The believers who were martyred in the Ditch did not lose. They won. They won the greatest possible victory.

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِي مِن تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْكَبِيرُ
"Indeed those who believed and did righteous deeds will have gardens beneath which rivers flow — that is the GREAT ATTAINMENT." (85:11)

⚡ LEVEL 5 — ALLAH'S GRIP IS SEVERE (85:12-20)
🎬 The Severe Grip — Click to reveal verse

The surah now pivots to Allah's response to the oppressors: "Inna batsha Rabbika la-shadid." — Indeed the GRIP/SEIZURE of your Lord is SEVERE.

"Batsh" is a powerful word — the seizing, grasping, taking hold. When Allah takes hold of an oppressor, His grip is "shadid" — severe, intense, inescapable. History proves this:

Pharaoh (Fir'awn) — with his vast army, his divine claims, his absolute power in Egypt — was destroyed. He drowned in the very sea he commanded. Thamud — the powerful people who carved mountains into dwellings — were wiped out by a single blast when they killed the prophet's she-camel.

And regarding the persecutors of the believers: "Wa Allahu min wara'ihim muhit" (85:20) — Allah surrounds/encompasses them from behind. They think they are free, powerful, beyond accountability. But Allah's knowledge and power completely surrounds them. There is no escape from His muhit (encompassing power).

For believers facing oppression, this verse provides certainty: you are not forgotten, the oppressor is not free. Allah's grip will come.

إِنَّ بَطْشَ رَبِّكَ لَشَدِيدٌ ۩ إِنَّهُ هُوَ يُبْدِئُ وَيُعِيدُ ۩ وَاللَّهُ مِن وَرَائِهِم مُّحِيطٌ
"Indeed the grip of your Lord is severe — Indeed it is He who begins and repeats — and Allah from behind them is encompassing." (85:12-13,20)

📜 LEVEL 6 — THE PRESERVED TABLET (85:21-22)
🎬 The Glorious Quran — Click to reveal verse

The surah concludes with a triumphant declaration that answers all persecution with one unassailable fact:

"Bal huwa Quranun Majid — fi Lawhin Mahfudh."

"Bal" — Rather/but. A word of strong contrast and correction. Rather than what the persecutors thought — that they could silence, destroy, or eliminate the message — the TRUTH is:

"Quranun Majid" — A GLORIOUS QURAN. "Majid" from "majd" meaning glory, honour, nobility, greatness. Not a book that can be burned or silenced — but a Glorious Quran.

"Fi Lawhin Mahfudh" — In a PRESERVED TABLET. "Mahfudh" from "hafiza" — protected, preserved, kept safe. The Lawh al-Mahfudh is the primordial, original record of Allah's decree and all His revelations — preserved with Him, beyond all harm, before the universe existed.

The People of the Ditch burned the bodies of believers. The Makkans persecuted the early Muslims. But the Quran — the book for which they suffered — is PRESERVED perfectly in the most secure place in existence. Their suffering was not in vain. The Quran endures.

بَلْ هُوَ قُرْآنٌ مَّجِيدٌ ۩ فِي لَوْحٍ مَّحْفُوظٍ
"But this is a Glorious Quran — preserved in a Preserved Tablet." (85:21-22)
