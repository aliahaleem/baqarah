Ibn Ameen says:
'Qul a'udhu bi-rabb il-falaq' — Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the DAYBREAK! Al-Falaq is the splitting of the morning light — a powerful, vivid image. We seek refuge in the One who COMMANDS light to break through darkness!
🌅 LEVEL 2 — LORD OF THE DAYBREAK (113:1-2)
🎬 A'udhu bi-rabb il-falaq — Click to reveal verse

Why specifically the "Lord of the Daybreak"? Because dawn (al-falaq) is the moment when Allah commands light to split through and overcome darkness. By seeking refuge in the Lord who controls this — we are reminding ourselves: He conquers all darkness.

The first evil we seek refuge from is general: "min sharri ma khalaq" — from the evil of WHATEVER He created. This covers everything — jinn, humans, animals, diseases, disasters.

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۩ مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
"Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak — from the evil of what He created." (113:1-2)

✨ LEVEL 5 — AL-FALAQ COMPLETE (113:1-5)
🎬 Protection Surah — Click to reveal verse

Al-Falaq and An-Nas together are called Al-Mu'awwidhatayn — the Two Protection Surahs. The Prophet ﷺ recited both after every Fajr and Maghrib prayer, and before sleeping — blowing on his hands and wiping his body.

Together they cover: external evils (Al-Falaq) and internal spiritual harm — the whispering of Shaytan (An-Nas).

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ
"Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak." (113:1)
