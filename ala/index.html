🌿 LEVEL 1 — GLORIFY THE MOST HIGH (87:1-5)
🎬 The Glorification — Click to reveal verse

Surah Al-Ala opens with a direct command to the Prophet ﷺ — and through him, to all believers: "Sabbihi isma Rabbika al-A'la!" — GLORIFY the name of your Lord, the Most High!

The word "Sabbihi" is the command form of "sabbaha" — from the root of "SubhanAllah." When the Prophet ﷺ received this surah, he commanded the believers to say "Subhana Rabbiya al-A'la" in sujood (prostration). Every Muslim says these words multiple times in every salah — directly reciting the first verse of this surah.

Then three pairs of divine actions are listed: Khalaqa fa-sawwa (created and proportioned), Qaddara fa-hada (destined and guided), Akhraja al-mar'a fa-ja'alahu ghuthan ahwa (brought out the pasture then made it dark debris). Each pair is complete — the creation and its proportion, the destiny and its guidance, the growth and the decay. Allah's actions are whole and perfect in every pair.

سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الْأَعْلَى ۩ الَّذِي خَلَقَ فَسَوَّىٰ ۩ وَالَّذِي قَدَّرَ فَهَدَىٰ ۩ وَالَّذِي أَخْرَجَ الْمَرْعَىٰ ۩ فَجَعَلَهُ غُثَاءً أَحْوَىٰ
"Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High — who created and proportioned — who destined and guided — who brought out the pasture — then made it dark debris." (87:1-5)

📿 LEVEL 2 — WE WILL MAKE YOU RECITE (87:6-7)
🎬 The Promise of Memory — Click to reveal verse

After the opening glorification and description of Allah's actions, the surah makes an extraordinary promise: "Sanuqri'uka fa-la tansa."

WE will make you RECITE — and you will NOT FORGET. The subject is Allah ("We" — the royal "We" of divine majesty). The Prophet ﷺ used to rush to repeat the revelation as it came, worried he would forget. Allah addresses this worry directly: don't rush — We are depositing it into your memory.

This verse is the Quranic explanation for one of Islam's most remarkable features: the oral preservation of the Quran. The Prophet ﷺ memorised it entirely, taught it to the companions, and across 1400 years millions have memorised the entire Quran — the only book in human history with such a living oral tradition of preservation.

The exception: "Illa ma sha' Allah" — except what Allah wills. This covers the phenomenon of abrogated verses — some revelations were later superseded and their memory allowed to fade, by Allah's design, not the Prophet's failure.

سَنُقْرِئُكَ فَلَا تَنسَىٰ ۩ إِلَّا مَا شَاءَ اللَّهُ ۚ إِنَّهُ يَعْلَمُ الْجَهْرَ وَمَا يَخْفَىٰ
"We will make you recite, and you will not forget — except what Allah wills. Indeed He knows the declared and what is hidden." (87:6-7)

🤲 LEVEL 3 — WHO FEARS AND WHO AVOIDS (87:9-13)
🎬 Two Types of Hearts — Click to reveal verse

After promising to preserve the Quran, the surah addresses the reception of the reminder. The Prophet ﷺ is told: "Fa-dhakkir in nafa'at al-dhikra" — So remind, if the reminder is beneficial.

Two responses are described: "Sayyadhdhakkaru man yakhsha" — The one who FEARS Allah with AWE will be reminded and benefit. "Yakhsha" — the awe-filled fear that comes from knowledge of Allah's greatness. This fear is like an open door — the reminder enters and transforms.

The contrast: "Wa yatajannabahu al-ashqa" — And the most wretched will AVOID it. "Yatajannaba" — to carefully stay away from, to dodge and evade. Not passive non-hearing — but active avoidance. The "most wretched" (al-ashqa) is the one who sees the reminder and deliberately sidesteps it.

This person: "Alladhi yasla al-nar al-kubra" — who will enter the GREAT fire. "Al-kubra" — the greatest. And in it: "Thumma la yamutu fiha wa la yahya" — neither dying in it nor living. A state worse than death and worse than life — suspended in punishment.

فَذَكِّرْ إِن نَّفَعَتِ الذِّكْرَىٰ ۩ سَيَذَّكَّرُ مَن يَخْشَىٰ ۩ وَيَتَجَنَّبُهَا الْأَشْقَى
"So remind, if the reminder should benefit — the one who fears will be reminded — and the most wretched will avoid it." (87:9-11)

✨ LEVEL 4 — PURIFY AND SUCCEED (87:14-15)
🎬 The Formula for Success — Click to reveal verse

After describing the two types — the one who fears (and benefits) and the one who avoids (and is destroyed) — the surah presents the formula for success: "Qad aflaha man tazakka — wa dhakara isma Rabbih fa-salla."

HE HAS CERTAINLY SUCCEEDED who PURIFIES himself. "Qad aflaha" — certainly, definitively succeeded. The word "falah" encompasses all dimensions of success: worldly and otherworldly, material and spiritual. "Tazakka" — purified himself from shirk, wrong beliefs, and spiritual diseases of the heart.

"Wa dhakara isma Rabbih" — and mentioned/remembered the name of his Lord. "Dhikr" is more than reciting words — it is keeping the consciousness of Allah alive in the heart throughout the day. Constant remembrance of "Rabbih" — my Lord — establishes the relationship.

"Fa-salla" — and then prayed. Salah is the external expression of the internal purification and remembrance. Together these three form a complete cycle: purify the inner, fill it with dhikr, express it through salah. This is the path of certain success.

قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن تَزَكَّىٰ ۩ وَذَكَرَ اسْمَ اللَّهِ فَصَلَّىٰ
"He has certainly succeeded who purifies himself — and mentions the name of his Lord and prays." (87:14-15)

⚡ LEVEL 5 — DUNYA VS HEREAFTER (87:16-17)
🎬 The Two Choices — Click to reveal verse

The surah now exposes the root problem: "Bal tu'thirun al-hayat al-dunya." But you PREFER the life of this world.

Not just "you live in the world" — you PREFER it. "Tu'thirun" — you give it priority, you choose it, you value it above what comes after. This is the disease: misplaced priority.

Then the correction comes immediately: "Wa al-akhiratu khayrun wa abqa." The Hereafter is BETTER (khayrun) AND MORE LASTING (abqa).

"Khayr" — better, higher quality, more beneficial. The pleasures of the Hereafter are incomparably superior to even the best moments of this world. "Abqa" — more remaining, more lasting, more permanent. This world: a few decades at most. The Hereafter: eternal.

There's also a beautiful linguistic point: "Al-dunya" literally means "the lowest" or "the nearest." And the surah opened by glorifying "Al-A'la" — the Highest! We are choosing the lowest when we could be orienting toward the Highest.

بَلْ تُؤْثِرُونَ الْحَيَاةَ الدُّنْيَا ۩ وَالْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌ وَأَبْقَىٰ
"But you prefer the life of this world — while the Hereafter is better and more lasting." (87:16-17)

📜 LEVEL 6 — IN THE EARLIEST SCROLLS (87:18-19)
🎬 Scrolls of Ibrahim and Musa — Click to reveal verse

The surah closes with a declaration that gives the entire message a universal, timeless dimension: "Inna hadha la-fi al-suhuf al-ula — suhuf Ibrahim wa Musa."

Indeed this (the message of this surah) is in the EARLIEST SCRIPTURES — the scrolls of Ibrahim and the scrolls of Musa.

This is remarkable. The command to glorify Allah, to purify oneself, to pray, to prefer the Hereafter over this world — these are not new teachings of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. They were in the very first revelations given to prophets. Ibrahim received this message. Musa received this message. Now the final prophet Muhammad ﷺ receives and completes it.

This establishes the unity of divine guidance across all prophets. The core message has never changed: Know your Lord, glorify Him, purify yourself, prefer eternity over the temporary. What changes are the laws and practices — what never changes is the orientation toward Allah and the Hereafter.

إِنَّ هَٰذَا لَفِي الصُّحُفِ الْأُولَىٰ ۩ صُحُفِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ
"Indeed this is in the earliest scriptures — the scriptures of Ibrahim and Musa." (87:18-19)
