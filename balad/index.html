🕋 LEVEL 1 — THE SACRED CITY (90:1-4)
🎬 Mecca — Click to reveal verse

Surah Al-Balad opens with Allah swearing by Mecca — the most sacred city on earth. "La uqsimu bi-hadhal-balad" — I do swear by this city! The "la" here is an emphatic particle, intensifying the oath rather than negating it.

Then comes a remarkable clause: "Wa-anta hillun bi-hadhal-balad" — and you are free of restriction in this city. This is addressed to the Prophet ﷺ. Despite Mecca being a city where warfare was normally forbidden, Allah was indicating that He would make it lawful for the Prophet ﷺ to enter it on the Day of Conquest.

The third oath: "Wa-walidin wa ma walad" — by the father and what he fathered. This refers to Ibrahim (AS) and his descendants — the chain of prophets that followed him.

Then the statement: "Laqad khalaqnal-insan fi kabad" — We have certainly created man in hardship! "Kabad" means difficulty, struggle, striving — the chest compressed under effort. Life was not designed to be easy. It was designed to test, to build, to purify.

لَا أُقْسِمُ بِهَٰذَا الْبَلَدِ ۩ وَأَنتَ حِلٌّ بِهَٰذَا الْبَلَدِ ۩ لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنسَانَ فِي كَبَدٍ
"I swear by this city — and you are free of restriction in this city — We have certainly created man in hardship." (90:1-4)

👁️ LEVEL 2 — EYES, TONGUE & TWO PATHS (90:8-10)
🎬 Two Highways — Click to reveal verse

Before describing the two paths, Allah reminds man of the gifts he was given for the journey. "Alam naj'al lahu 'aynayn" — Have We not made for him two eyes? These eyes can see the signs of Allah everywhere — in creation, in history, in oneself.

"Wa-lisanan wa-shafatayn" — And a tongue and two lips? The tongue is the most powerful instrument of good and evil. With it you can recite Quran, speak truth, comfort the grieving. Or you can lie, backbite, and harm. The lips shape every word.

"Wa-hadaynahu al-najdayn" — And We guided him to the two highways! "Al-najdayn" — two elevated, visible roads. Not a hidden path — both were clearly shown. The good path and the evil path were both made unmistakably clear to man.

The tragedy (as the surah continues to show): despite all these gifts and clear guidance — man still doesn't take the steep righteous path.

أَلَمْ نَجْعَل لَّهُ عَيْنَيْنِ ۩ وَلِسَانًا وَشَفَتَيْنِ ۩ وَهَدَيْنَاهُ النَّجْدَيْنِ
"Have We not made for him two eyes, and a tongue and two lips? And We guided him to the two highways." (90:8-10)

🏔️ LEVEL 3 — AL-AQABAH (90:11-16)
🎬 The Steep Path — Click to reveal verse

"Fala-qtahama al-aqabah" — But he has not attempted the steep path! (90:11) The verb "iqtahama" means to throw oneself into something difficult, to assault a challenge with determination. Al-aqabah is the steep mountain path — the harder road.

Then a dramatic question: "Wa ma adraka mal-aqabah?" — And what would make you know what the aqabah is? This question builds suspense — as if to say: it's not what you might think. The definition is not a ritual act alone.

"Fakku raqabah" — It is the freeing of a slave! (90:13) In 7th-century Arabia, this was the most radical, most costly act of social justice. A slave had no rights, no future. To free one was to restore a human being's entire existence.

Then: "Aw it'amun fi yawmin dhi masghaba" — Or feeding on a day of severe hunger! (90:14) Not feeding when you have plenty — but feeding on a day when YOU are also hungry. That is when generosity truly tests your heart.

The recipients: "Yatiman dha maqraba" — an orphan who is your close relative (90:15). "Aw miskeenan dha matraba" — or a person in such extreme poverty they are "matraba" — literally face-down in the dust. (90:16)

فَلَا اقْتَحَمَ الْعَقَبَةَ ۩ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الْعَقَبَةُ ۩ فَكُّ رَقَبَةٍ ۩ أَوْ إِطْعَامٌ فِي يَوْمٍ ذِي مَسْغَبَةٍ
"But he has not attempted the steep path — and what would make you know what the steep path is? It is the freeing of a slave — or feeding on a day of severe hunger." (90:11-14)

🍲 LEVEL 4 — ACTS OF THE AQABAH (90:13-17)
🎬 Feeding the Poor — Click to reveal verse

The surah defines the aqabah through concrete acts of social justice and generosity. These are not vague — they are specific, demanding, and costly.

1. Fakku raqabah — Freeing a slave. The most expensive act of liberation. In the Islamic tradition, freeing a slave was the highest form of expiation for many sins, showing just how valued this act was in Allah's sight.

2. Feeding an orphan near relative — "Yatiman dha maqraba" — (90:15). The orphan who is your own family member. You have double obligation: as a fellow human being AND as a relative. And "on a day of severe hunger" — when it costs you personally.

3. Feeding the utterly destitute — "Miskeenan dha matraba" — (90:16). "Dha matraba" — one who is face-down in dirt, utterly stripped of everything. The most extreme poverty, with no family, no resources, no help.

4. Urging mercy — Not just doing these acts yourself, but "tawassaw bil-marhama" — mutually advising each other towards mercy (90:17). Social justice is a COMMUNITY project. When each person urges the next, the whole society climbs the aqabah together.

يَتِيمًا ذَا مَقْرَبَةٍ ۩ أَوْ مِسْكِينًا ذَا مَتْرَبَةٍ ۩ ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْمَرْحَمَةِ
"An orphan of near relationship — or a needy person in misery — then he was of those who believed and urged each other to mercy." (90:15-17)

⚖️ LEVEL 5 — TWO TYPES OF PEOPLE (90:17-20)
🎬 Two Destinies — Click to reveal verse

The surah concludes by dividing humanity into two groups based on their choices.

The first group: "Thumma kana minal-ladhina amanu wa-tawassaw bis-sabri wa-tawassaw bil-marhama" — Then he was of those who believed and urged each other to patience and urged each other to mercy. (90:17)

Note the word "thumma" — then. It comes AFTER all the acts. Belief alone is not enough — it must be expressed through action (the aqabah) AND supported by community (urging patience and mercy to each other). Three elements: belief, patient perseverance, and mutual compassion.

Their designation: "Ula'ika ashaab al-maymana" — Those are the Companions of the Right Hand. (90:18) "Maymana" means blessing and right-handedness — they will receive their books of deeds in their right hand on the Day of Judgement, and they will enter Jannah.

The second group: "Walladhina kafaru bi-ayatina hum ashaab al-mash'ama" — Those who rejected Our signs — they are the Companions of the Left. (90:19) "Mash'ama" means misery and the left side — they will receive their books in their left hand.

"Alayhim narun mu'sada" — Over them fire is closing in completely. (90:20) "Mu'sada" means sealed, shut tight — no escape possible.

ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْمَرْحَمَةِ ۩ أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْمَيْمَنَةِ
"Then he was of those who believed and urged each other to patience and urged each other to mercy — those are the Companions of the Right." (90:17-18)

✨ LEVEL 6 — COMPANIONS OF RIGHT AND LEFT (90:17-20)
🎬 Final Judgement — Click to reveal verse

The final verses of Surah Al-Balad make it crystal clear: every human being will end up in one of two groups.

The Companions of the Right (Ashaab al-Maymana) are those who combined three things: iman (belief), tawasi bis-sabr (urging each other to patience), and tawasi bil-marhama (urging each other to mercy). The community aspect is striking — Islam is not a solo journey. We help each other climb the mountain.

The Companions of the Left (Ashaab al-Mash'ama) are those who rejected Allah's signs — not through ignorance alone, but through wilful rejection ("kafaru bi-ayatina"). For them: "Alayhim narun mu'sada" — fire is closing over them completely. "Mu'sada" comes from "wasada" — to close a lid tightly. There is no way out.

Surah Al-Balad is a call to action: you were created in hardship. Allah gave you eyes, tongue, and showed you both paths. The aqabah is steep — but it leads to Jannah. The easy path of selfishness leads to sealed fire. The choice was made clear. Now choose.

وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِآيَاتِنَا هُمْ أَصْحَابُ الْمَشْأَمَةِ ۩ عَلَيْهِمْ نَارٌ مُّؤْصَدَةٌ
"Those who rejected Our signs — they are the Companions of the Left — over them fire is closing in." (90:19-20)
