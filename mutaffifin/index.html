⚖️ LEVEL 1 — WOE TO THE DEFRAUDERS (83:1-6)
🎬 The Unequal Scales — Click to reveal verse

This surah was among the first to be revealed after the Prophet ﷺ migrated to Madinah. The marketplace there was known for dishonest practices — merchants who would carefully take a full measure when purchasing from others, but when selling, their scales would tip just slightly in their favour.

Allah begins immediately: "Waylun lil-mutaffifin!" — Woe to the defrauders! "Tatfif" comes from a root meaning "the edge, the rim" — giving just a hair short of the full amount. Not dramatically stealing — just slightly, systematically, always in their favour.

The surah then poses a devastating question: "A-la yadhunnu ula'ika annahum mab'uthun — li-yawmin 'adhim?" — Do they not think they will be RAISED for a GREAT Day? The cheating reveals an underlying belief that there will be no accounting. If you truly believed your every gram would be weighed on That Day — you would weigh every gram perfectly for others.

وَيْلٌ لِّلْمُطَفِّفِينَ ۩ الَّذِينَ إِذَا اكْتَالُوا عَلَى النَّاسِ يَسْتَوْفُونَ ۩ وَإِذَا كَالُوهُمْ أَو وَّزَنُوهُمْ يُخْسِرُونَ
"Woe to the defrauders — those who, when they take a measure from people, take it in full — but when they give by measure or weight, they give less." (83:1-3)

📕 LEVEL 2 — THE BOOK OF SIJJIN (83:7-9)
🎬 The Lowest Record — Click to reveal verse

After describing the defrauders, Allah declares: "Kalla!" — No! Stop! There is complete accountability coming. And then: "Inna kitab al-fujjar la-fi Sijjin."

The record of the wicked (al-Fujjar) is preserved in Sijjin — a word related to "sijn" (prison). Scholars say it is in the seventh earth, the lowest possible location — below all creation, the most degraded and confined place.

Then the rhetorical question: "Wa ma adraka ma Sijjin?" — What would make you know what Sijjin is? The question signals: its awfulness is beyond description. It is then defined simply as "kitab marqum" — a clearly inscribed, marked record. Sealed. Specific. Permanent. Every deed filed away in the worst place imaginable.

كَلَّا إِنَّ كِتَابَ الْفُجَّارِ لَفِي سِجِّينٍ ۩ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا سِجِّينٌ ۩ كِتَابٌ مَّرْقُومٌ
"No indeed! The record of the wicked is in Sijjin — and what would make you know what Sijjin is? — A marked record." (83:7-9)

📗 LEVEL 3 — THE BOOK OF ILLIYYIN (83:18-21)
🎬 The Highest Record — Click to reveal verse

After describing the deniers of Yawm al-Din (83:10-17), the surah pivots completely with another "Kalla!" — No! For the righteous, the story is entirely different.

"Inna kitab al-abrar la-fi Illiyyin" — The record of the righteous (al-Abrar) is in Illiyyin. From "\'ala" — high. The highest possible register. Scholars say it is above the seventh heaven, in the most elevated location, near the Throne of Allah.

The same rhetorical question: "Wa ma adraka ma Illiyyun?" — But now signalling something magnificent rather than terrible. Then the same definition: "kitab marqum" — a marked record. The same TYPE of thing — but in the most honoured location in existence.

And then something unique — "Yash-haduhu al-muqarrabun" — witnessed by those brought closest to Allah. The most noble angels attend and witness the records of the righteous. Your good deeds are honoured in the most prestigious possible way.

كَلَّا إِنَّ كِتَابَ الْأَبْرَارِ لَفِي عِلِّيِّينَ ۩ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا عِلِّيُّونَ ۩ كِتَابٌ مَّرْقُومٌ ۩ يَشْهَدُهُ الْمُقَرَّبُونَ
"The record of the righteous is in Illiyyin — and what would make you know what Illiyyin is? — A marked record, witnessed by those brought near." (83:18-21)

🌿 LEVEL 4 — THE RIGHTEOUS IN BLISS (83:22-26)
🎬 Couches of Bliss — Click to reveal verse

The surah paints a vivid picture of what the Abrar (righteous) receive: "Inna al-abrar la-fi na'im — 'ala al-ara'iki yandhuruna" — they are in bliss, reclining on raised couches, looking.

"Taf'rifu fi wujuhihim nahrat al-na'im" — you would RECOGNISE in their faces the radiance of bliss. "Nahrah" means freshness, radiance, luminosity. Joy so complete and deep that it literally shines on their faces. It is not just internal happiness — it radiates outward visibly.

They are given to drink of "rahiq makhtum" — sealed pure nectar. "Makhtum" means sealed — completely pure, untampered, reserved only for them. The seal of this drink? MUSK (83:26). The most precious scent.

Then the greatest divine invitation in the whole Quran: "Wa fi dhalika fal-yatanafas al-mutanafisun" — For THIS — let those who want to compete, COMPETE! Not for wealth. Not for status. Not for followers. But for a place among the Abrar, with records in Illiyyin, on couches of bliss, with radiant faces and sealed nectar. THIS is the real competition.

إِنَّ الْأَبْرَارَ لَفِي نَعِيمٍ ۩ عَلَى الْأَرَائِكِ يَنظُرُونَ ۩ تَعْرِفُ فِي وُجُوهِهِمْ نَضْرَةَ النَّعِيمِ ۩ يُسْقَوْنَ مِن رَّحِيقٍ مَّخْتُومٍ ۩ خِتَامُهُ مِسْكٌ
"Indeed the righteous are in bliss — on couches, looking — you recognise in their faces the radiance of bliss — they are given a drink of sealed pure nectar — its seal is musk." (83:22-26)

🔄 LEVEL 5 — THE GREAT REVERSAL (83:29-36)
🎬 The Roles Reversed — Click to reveal verse

The surah closes with a precise mirror image — what happened in this world will be exactly reversed in the next.

In this world (83:29-33): those who committed crimes "kanu min alladhina amanu yadhakun" — used to LAUGH at the believers. When they passed the believers on the street, they would "yataghama zu" — wink and nudge each other mockingly. They would return to their families laughing. They called believers "dhallun" — misguided people.

On That Day (83:34): "Fa-al-yawm alladhina amanu min al-kuffar yadhakun" — TODAY those who believed are laughing at the disbelievers. The laughter returns. The same energy, completely reversed.

The surah closes with a final rhetorical question: "Hal thuwwiba al-kuffaru ma kanu yaf'alun?" — Were the disbelievers rewarded for what they used to do? Yes — completely, perfectly. Every mockery returned. Every sneer accounted for. The scales of justice are always balanced.

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ أَجْرَمُوا كَانُوا مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا يَضْحَكُونَ ۩ فَالْيَوْمَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنَ الْكُفَّارِ يَضْحَكُونَ
"Indeed those who committed crimes used to laugh at those who believed — but today those who believed are laughing at the disbelievers." (83:29,34)

📊 LEVEL 6 — RESURRECTION AND JUSTICE (83:4-6)
🎬 The Day of Rising — Click to reveal verse

Surah Al-Mutaffifin contains a remarkable logical argument. The defrauders cheat in their weights. Allah's response is not "this is financially wrong" — it is: "Do they not think they will be RAISED, for a GREAT Day?"

The argument is: Your cheating reveals your belief system. If you truly believed you would face a perfect, precise accounting of every gram on the Day of Judgment, you would weigh every gram perfectly for others. The fact that you cheat reveals a hidden conviction that there will be no accounting.

This is the Day when "Yaqumu al-nasu li-Rabbi al-'alamin" — the people will stand for the Lord of all the worlds (83:6). The word "yaqumu" — they will STAND. Upright. Before their Lord. No sitting, no reclining, no comfort. Standing.

The entire surah — from marketplace fraud to Sijjin and Illiyyin to bliss and Hell — is built on one foundation: the certainty of resurrection and perfect accountability. Believe in it deeply, and every aspect of your life changes.

أَلَا يَظُنُّ أُولَٰئِكَ أَنَّهُم مَّبْعُوثُونَ ۩ لِيَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ ۩ يَوْمَ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
"Do they not think that they will be resurrected — for a Great Day — the Day when people will stand for the Lord of all the worlds?" (83:4-6)
