🌅 LEVEL 1 — THE OATHS OF ALLAH (89:1-5)
🎬 The Dawn Oaths — Click to reveal verse

Surah Al-Fajr opens with a series of cosmic oaths. Allah swears by four things — each pointing to His creation and the cycles of time He has established.

"Wal-fajri" — By the Dawn! The Fajr is the time when night surrenders to light. It is the time of the most blessed prayer — when the angels of the night and the angels of the day both gather to witness the believer who rises before sunrise for Allah.

"Wa layalin 'ashr" — By the ten nights! Scholars unanimously identify these as the first ten nights of Dhul-Hijjah. The Prophet ﷺ said: "There are no days in which righteous deeds are more beloved to Allah than these ten days." This includes the Day of Arafah — the greatest day of the year — and Eid al-Adha.

"Wash-shaf'i wal-watr" — By the even and the odd! Some scholars say this refers to all of creation (which is either paired or singular). Others say it refers specifically to the prayers: the even (Isha/Fajr with even rak'at) and the odd (Witr — one rak'ah). "Watr" reminds us of the Witr prayer — that beautiful final prayer before sleep.

All four oaths set up the gravity of what follows: the stories of destroyed civilisations and the lessons for those who think.

وَالْفَجْرِ ۩ وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍ ۩ وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ ۩ وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ
"By the dawn — and the ten nights — and the even and the odd — and the night when it passes." (89:1-4) — Four oaths pointing to time, cycles, and the sacred moments Allah has elevated above others.

🏛️ LEVEL 2 — THREE DESTROYED NATIONS (89:6-14)
🎬 Pillars of Iram — Click to reveal verse

After the oaths, Allah presents three historical warnings — civilisations that were far mightier than the Quraysh, yet were all destroyed for their transgression.

\'Aad of Iram — They were a towering people who built the legendary city of Iram with lofty pillars, "the like of which was never created in any land" (89:8). They were the mightiest in strength, yet their Prophet Hud (AS) was rejected. Allah sent upon them a violent, howling wind for seven nights and eight days, leaving them like hollow palm trunks.

Thamud — They carved magnificent homes from the mountains of Wadi al-Qura. Their Prophet Salih (AS) was given a miraculous she-camel as a sign. When they killed it in arrogance, the punishment came within three days. The land was struck and they were annihilated.

Pharaoh — Master of the tent-pegs (stakes used to crucify people). He had unparalleled power and wealth, declared himself divine, and tortured the believers. He drowned in the sea while pursuing Musa (AS).

The verdict: "Inna rabbaka la bil-mirsad" — Indeed your Lord is ever watchful. (89:14) — No transgressor escapes.

أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ ۩ إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ ۩ الَّتِي لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلَادِ
"Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with \'Aad — of Iram with lofty pillars — the like of which was never created in the lands?" (89:6-8) — The greatness of \'Aad makes their destruction even more powerful as a lesson.

⚖️ LEVEL 3 — TESTS OF WEALTH AND POVERTY (89:15-20)
🎬 The Two Tests — Click to reveal verse

After showing the punishment of transgressors, Allah shifts to the present — to human psychology. He describes man's fundamental mistake in understanding tests.

"Fa-amm al-insan idha ma-btalahu rabbahu fa-akramahu wa-na''amahu fa-yaqul rabbi akraman" — As for man, when his Lord tests him by honouring him and giving him ease, he says: "My Lord has honoured me!" (89:15) — He sees wealth as a sign of divine approval.

"Wa-amma idha ma-btalahu faqadara alayhi rizqahu fa-yaqul rabbi ahanam" — But when He tests him by restricting his provision, he says: "My Lord has humiliated me!" (89:16) — He sees hardship as divine rejection.

Allah uses "Kalla!" — No! — to reject both conclusions. Neither wealth nor poverty is proof of Allah's love or anger. Both are tests. The question is: what do you do with what you've been given?

Then Allah lists what man SHOULD have done but didn't: honoured orphans, encouraged feeding the poor, and restrained his love of wealth.

فَأَمَّا الْإِنسَانُ إِذَا مَا ابْتَلَاهُ رَبُّهُ فَأَكْرَمَهُ وَنَعَّمَهُ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّي أَكْرَمَنِ
"As for man — when his Lord tests him and honours him with wealth, he says: My Lord has honoured me!" (89:15) — The error: thinking wealth equals honour. But in reality, it's a test of gratitude and generosity.

💔 LEVEL 4 — WHAT MAN DOES WRONG (89:17-20)
🎬 Four Wrongs — Click to reveal verse

After rejecting man's false conclusions, Allah lists the specific wrongs that stem from misunderstanding the purpose of wealth.

"Bal la tukrimon al-yatim" — No! You do not honour the orphan. (89:17) — The orphan is the most vulnerable member of society. "Honouring" (ikram) means more than not harming — it means actively treating them with dignity, love, and fairness.

"Wa la tahaddun ala ta'am al-miskin" — And you do not urge one another to feed the poor. (89:18) — Note: not just "you don't feed the poor" but you don't even ENCOURAGE others to! The communal responsibility is neglected.

"Wa ta'kulun al-turath aklan lamma" — And you consume inheritance, devouring it all greedily. (89:19) — "Lamma" means consuming everything without distinction — taking even what belongs to orphans and widows.

"Wa tuhibbon al-mal hubban jamman" — And you love wealth with an excessive, overwhelming love. (89:20) — "Jamm" means total, complete, overflowing love. When love of wealth becomes the driving force of life, everything else suffers.

كَلَّا ۖ بَل لَّا تُكْرِمُونَ الْيَتِيمَ ۩ وَلَا تَحَاضُّونَ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ الْمِسْكِينِ
"No! You do not honour the orphan — and you do not urge one another to feed the poor." (89:17-18) — Two social failures named side by side: ignoring the vulnerable and failing community responsibility.

💫 LEVEL 5 — THE DAY OF REGRET (89:21-26)
🎬 The Great Day — Click to reveal verse

After describing man's wrongs, Allah fast-forwards to the ultimate consequence — the Day of Judgement. The contrast is meant to shake the reader awake.

"Kalla idha dukkati al-ardu dakkan dakka" — No! When the earth is levelled completely. (89:21) — "Dakkan dakka" — the repetition intensifies it. The earth, which man trusted and upon which he built his plans and hoarded his wealth, will be utterly flattened.

"Wa ja'a rabbuka wal-malaku saffan saffa" — And your Lord comes, and the angels rank upon rank. (89:22) — The scholars have detailed discussions on how to understand this. What's clear: the Day of Judgement will be unlike anything in human experience. The angels come in perfect, organised rows.

"Wa ji'a yawma'idhin bi-jahannam" — And Jahannam is brought forth that Day. (89:23) — This is the moment of reality. The Hellfire is actually brought near to the gathering of people so all can see it.

"Yawma'idhin yatadhakkar al-insan wa anna lahu al-dhikra" — That Day, man will remember — but what good will the remembrance be to him? (89:23) — Too late.

"Ya laytani qaddamtu li-hayati!" — "Oh, I wish I had prepared for my life ahead!" (89:24) — The regret will be total and overwhelming. The word "hayati" — "for my life" — means the real life: the afterlife. He finally understands that THIS world was not the life — it was preparation for it.

يَقُولُ يَا لَيْتَنِي قَدَّمْتُ لِحَيَاتِي ۩ فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَّا يُعَذِّبُ عَذَابَهُ أَحَدٌ
"He will say: Oh, I wish I had prepared for my life ahead! — That Day, no one will punish as Allah punishes, and no one will bind as He binds." (89:24-26) — The regret is total. The punishment is unmatched.

🕊️ LEVEL 6 — THE TRANQUIL SOUL (89:27-30)
🎬 O Tranquil Soul — Click to reveal verse

After the frightening passage about the Day of Regret, Surah Al-Fajr ends with the most beautiful, comforting address in the entire Quran.

"Ya ayyatuha an-nafs al-mutma'inna" — O tranquil soul! (89:27) — "Mutma'inna" comes from "tuma'ninah" — complete inner peace, settled tranquility. This is a soul that has found rest in remembrance of Allah. As Allah says elsewhere: "Ala bi-dhikrillahi tatma'inn al-qulub" — Verily, in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest (13:28).

"Irji'i ila rabbiki radiyatan mardiyyah" — Return to your Lord, satisfied and pleasing! (89:28) — "Radiyatan" — satisfied with Allah's decree, with whatever Allah gave her. "Mardiyyah" — pleasing to Allah. Both directions: she is happy with Allah, and Allah is happy with her. This is the ultimate mutual satisfaction.

"Fadkhuli fi 'ibadi — Wadkhuli jannati" — Enter among My servants — and enter My Paradise! (89:29-30) — "My servants" — a special group. "My Paradise" — possessive form — Allah claims it as His, showing its supreme honour. Two commands, two invitations, two doors of honour.

This ending is not for everyone. It is for the soul that lived a life of worship, gratitude, patience, and submission. May Allah make our souls the mutma'inna. Ameen.

يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ۩ ارْجِعِي إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَّرْضِيَّةً ۩ فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبَادِي ۩ وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي
"O tranquil soul — return to your Lord, satisfied and pleasing — and enter among My servants — and enter My Paradise!" (89:27-30) — The most beautiful address in the Quran. May Allah call us by these words.
